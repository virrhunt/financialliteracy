Some quick terms to know before we start...

  • stocks = a stock, also known as equity, is a security that represents a particular fraction of ownership in a company
  • cryptocurrency = an encrypted decentralized digital currency. Transactions are recorded on the Blockchain, a coded ledger of exchanges (Most popular types: Bitcoin, Ethereum, Tether, BNB)
  • bonds = a fixed income investment where individuals lend money to a government or company at a certain interest rate for an amount of time; the government or company then repays lenders with interest in addition to the original face value of the bond
  • CD (certificate of deposit) = a type of savings account where you agree to leave your money in the bank for a set amount of time and earn a fixed interest rate. It usually pays more interest than a regular savings account, but if you take your money out early, youâ€™ll have to pay a penalty.

The graph above compares the total global asset growth of mutual funds a professionally managed investment company that pools money from many investors to purchase a variety of securities, such as stocks, bonds, and short-term debt and exchange-traded funds over a period between 1998 and 2023. Though the two investments are very similar, the chart demonstrates the recent growth of popularity in ETFs, A pooled investment security that can be bought and sold like an individual stock which differ from mutual funds in their trading window and management style. While mutual funds can only be traded once day after the markets close, ETFs can be bought and sold throughout the day, similar to regular stocks. Over the past 20 years, both types of investments have seen significant growth due their associations with low risk possibility of losing money or experiencing a financial loss due to a variety of factors and diversification, practice of spreading investments across different asset classes, industries, and regions to reduce risk and volatility
